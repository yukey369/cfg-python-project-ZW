Shanghai

Shanghai is a national historical and cultural city located in the Yangtze River Delta.
It is China's international economic, financial, trade, shipping, and technology innovation center.
In Shanghai, you can experience cutting-edge technology facilities, efficient transportation systems,
appreciation of antique historical sites, and taste of Chinese specialties.

Highlights in Shanghai









The Bund

Located on the Huangpu River in Huangpu District of Shanghai,
it is outside Huangpu Beach and is a historical and cultural block in China.
It is also the starting point for the old Shanghai concession area and the entire modern city of Shanghai.





Tianzifang

Before 1998, it was still a street market, and now it has become a gathering place for modern creativity, full of humanistic art.





Yuyuan

Located in the northeast of Shanghai's old city, it is a classical garden in the south of the Yangtze River.
It was built in the Jiajing and Wanli years of the Ming Dynasty and covers an area of more than 30 acres.





Nanjing Road

Nanjing Road in Shanghai is the first commercial street to be established after the opening of Shanghai.
The intertwining of tradition and modernity adds a different charm to this century-old street.
It is a window to Shanghai's opening to the outside world and a paradise for shoppers at home and abroad.
